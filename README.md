Report: Survey of Data Professional Breakdown

Tool Used: Microsoft Power BI
Total Survey Respondents: 630
Average Age of Respondents: 29.87 years

1. Introduction

This report presents insights derived from a Power BI dashboard titled “Survey of Data Professional Breakdown.” The dashboard analyzes survey responses from data professionals across different countries, job roles, genders, and skill preferences. The objective is to understand demographic distribution, salary trends, programming language preferences, and overall happiness levels related to work-life balance and salary.

2. Geographic Distribution of Survey Takers

The survey respondents are geographically diverse, with the highest participation coming from:

United States: 261 respondents

Other Countries: 224 respondents

India: 73 respondents

United Kingdom: 40 respondents

Canada: 32 respondents

Insight:
The United States dominates the survey participation, indicating that most insights—especially salary-related—are heavily influenced by the US job market. However, strong representation from other regions suggests global relevance.

3. Average Salary by Job Title

The dashboard highlights average salaries across different data-related roles:

Data Analyst: 21K (highest average salary)

Other Roles: 5K

Data Engineer: 2K

Student / Looking for Job: 2K

Data Scientist: 2K

Data Architect & Database Developer: Lowest reported averages

Insight:
Data Analysts appear to earn the highest average salary among respondents, which may reflect the high demand for analytics skills and strong entry-to-mid-level opportunities in the market.

4. Average Salary by Gender

Salary distribution by gender shows:

Male: 25.02K (73.67%)

Female: 8.94K (26.33%)

Insight:
There is a noticeable gender gap in salary distribution. Male respondents represent a significantly larger portion of higher average earnings, highlighting an ongoing gender imbalance within the data profession.

5. Favorite Programming Languages

The most preferred programming languages among respondents are:

Python: Most dominant (especially among Data Analysts)

R: Second most popular

Other Languages: Moderate usage

C/C++ and JavaScript: Limited preference

Java: Least preferred

Insight:
Python is clearly the industry-standard language for data professionals due to its versatility, ease of use, and strong ecosystem for data analysis, machine learning, and visualization.

6. Happiness Index Analysis
6.1 Happiness with Work-Life Balance

Average Score: 5.74 out of 10

This indicates a moderate level of satisfaction, suggesting that while respondents are not dissatisfied, there is room for improvement in balancing professional and personal life.

6.2 Happiness with Salary

Average Score: 4.27 out of 10

This lower score suggests that many data professionals feel underpaid or not fully satisfied with their compensation, despite working in a high-demand field.

7. Key Findings Summary

The majority of respondents are young professionals, with an average age under 30.

Data Analysts earn the highest reported average salary among surveyed roles.

Python is the most widely used and preferred programming language.

There is a significant gender gap in salary distribution.

Overall happiness with work-life balance is moderate, while salary satisfaction is relatively low.

8. Conclusion

The Power BI dashboard effectively visualizes critical aspects of the data professional landscape. It highlights strong demand for analytical roles, the dominance of Python as a core skill, and important concerns around salary satisfaction and gender inequality. These insights can be valuable for aspiring data professionals, employers, and policymakers to better understand workforce trends and areas requiring improvement.
